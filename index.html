Eating

The delicious food you'll enjoy in Malaysia is a reflection of the cuisines of the country’s Malay, Chinese and Indian communities. Zone in on the big cities – Kuala Lumpur, George Town, Melaka – for the best selection of dishes. There are fewer culinary choices outside urban areas, where staple meals of mee goreng (fried noodles) and nasi goreng (fried rice) predominate. Vegetarian dishes are usually available at both Malay and Indian cafes, but are hardly sighted at kedai kopi (coffee shops). You can also find a wonderful array of fruit and vegetables at markets.

Shopping

Multicoloured batik prints and ikat weaves, tribal woodcarvings and rattan baskets, beautiful objects crafted from silver and pewter, giant kites and fascinating shadow puppets – all this and more are among the many unique items on offer in Malaysia's shops and markets. The country's many air-conditioned malls are good places to cool off, hangout and indulge in retail therapy among a host of local and international brand stores.

  • Central Market Art deco building hosting top selection of arts and crafts in Kuala Lumpur.
  • Jonker Walk Night Market Melaka City's weekly extravaganza of street food and shopping.
  • Unique Penang Charming artworks, prints and postcards in George Town.
  • Kong Heng Square Artisan Market Craft goods hub of Ipoh's old town.
  • Museum Cafe & Shop Kuching's one-stop shop for local textiles, organic soaps, books and artworks.
